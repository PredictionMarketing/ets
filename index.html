🔧 2. Service Lines — Deep Analysis

2A. Vehicle Forensics / Crash Data Retrieval (CDR)

Their primary advertised service. They claim to extract and analyze Event Data Recorder (EDR) data from vehicles involved in accidents.

Source: etsintelligence.com/vehicle-forensics

What They Claim:

  • CDR data extraction and analysis from vehicle "black boxes"
  • Pre-crash data: speed, throttle, brake, steering input
  • Crash data: impact forces, velocity changes, airbag/seatbelt deployment
  • Post-crash data: duration, secondary impacts, system status
  • Expert testimony capabilities
  • "Certified CDR technicians" on staff
  • Work with attorneys, insurance companies, and law enforcement

How They Position It:

They frame CDR work as the objective, data-driven alternative to traditional accident reconstruction. Their messaging emphasizes that EDR data "doesn't lie" — positioning it as unimpeachable truth in disputed claims. This targets attorneys, insurance adjusters, and fleet managers.

🚩 Red Flag: Their website does not specify which certifications their analysts hold (Bosch CDR Technician? CDR Analyst? Collision Safety Institute?). Legitimate firms prominently display specific credential names and numbers. The phrasing "certified CDR technicians" is technically vague — certified by whom?

🛡️ 12. Operational Guardrails & Rules Addendum

⚡ Hard Boundaries for All Content & Communications

Every piece of content — website copy, emails, ads, call scripts, AI-generated material — must comply with these rules. No exceptions.

Section 1: Identity & Representation

Rule Directive
1.1 Never name ETS Intelligence in any public-facing content. Not on the website. Not in emails. Not in ads. Not on calls.
1.2 Never claim to be ETS Intelligence, an employee of ETS, or a private investigator. We are an authorized sales partner.
1.3 Never reveal the total number of authorized sellers (five) or reference the existence of other sellers.
1.4 Refer to the service provider in general terms only: "our partner," "our intelligence provider," "our data source."

Section 2: Pricing & Financial Terms

Rule Directive
2.1 Never quote specific pricing in any content or communication. Nowhere.
2.2 Pricing variables (volume, market, severity, vehicle type) are discussed only in qualified consultations.
2.3 If asked about pricing: "Pricing depends on your specific needs. That's exactly what the consultation covers. Let's get you booked."
2.4 Never offer discounts, promotions, or special pricing. Pricing decisions belong to ETS.

Section 3: Legal Boundaries

Rule Directive
3.1 We are not lawyers. Never make legal statements, give legal advice, or imply legal expertise.
3.2 Legal-adjacent language must be general. ✅ "Law firms use accident reports to identify potential clients." ❌ "These reports will help you win cases."
3.3 Never make claims about legal outcomes, case results, or settlement amounts.
3.4 If asked a legal question: "Great question — better answered by the legal experts on our partner's side. Let me get you connected."
3.5 All content must include appropriate disclaimers where legally advisable.

Section 4: Competitive Positioning

Rule Directive
4.1 Never name competitors. Acknowledge and redirect to our strengths.
4.2 Lead with coverage (licensed PIs in every state) and data quality (fresh, accurate, synopsis format, high-intent targeting).
4.3 If a suspected fellow authorized seller is mentioned, treat them as any competitor — don't engage, redirect.
4.4 Never make comparative claims we can't verify ("We're the only ones who..." etc.) unless confirmed by ETS.

Section 5: The Singular Goal

Rule Directive
5.1 Every piece of content has one objective: book a qualified consultation call with our partner.
5.2 We qualify prospects. We do not close deals, negotiate terms, or make commitments.
5.3 "Sufficiently" = enough info that the prospect feels confident taking the next step, but not so much they skip the call.
5.4 If a prospect is clearly unqualified, disqualify politely. Our partner's time is valuable.
